Eminem

Eminem, born Marshall Bruce Mathers III on October 17, 1972, is an American rapper, songwriter, record producer, and actor. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest and most influential rappers of all time. Eminem's career began in the underground rap scene of Detroit, Michigan, where he gained attention for his raw and emotionally charged lyrics.

In 1999, Eminem released his major-label debut album, "The Slim Shady LP," which catapulted him to fame with hits like "My Name Is" and "Guilty Conscience." He continued to achieve commercial success and critical acclaim with albums like "The Marshall Mathers LP," "The Eminem Show," and "Recovery." Eminem's music often tackles personal struggles, societal issues, and his own experiences, resonating with millions of fans around the world.
